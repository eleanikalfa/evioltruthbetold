Common supplement myths

✕ Myth

Is natural always safe?

Just because something comes from nature does not mean your body agrees with it. Hemlock is natural. So is arsenic.

≈ Partly true

Do more vitamins mean better health?

The relationship between dose and benefit is more complicated than most labels suggest. More is not always better.

✕ Myth

Can supplements replace a balanced diet?

Supplements support health. They do not replicate the complexity of whole food nutrition. Not even close.

Read the science →
✕ Myth

Do vitamins boost energy instantly?

Energy is a complex metabolic process. Supplements play a supporting role — not the starring one the label implies.

≈ Partly true

Do supplements work the same for everyone?

Age, diet, genetics, and existing conditions all affect how your body uses supplements. What works online may not work for you.

Ask Before You Add

Your pharmacist.
Not your feed.

Greece has more than 12,000 pharmacists. They are trained, regulated, and free to consult. Most people have one within five minutes of where they live. And yet most supplement decisions are made alone, at a checkout screen, based on a post someone liked.

01
Bring your question

You can ask about a specific product, an ingredient, or a symptom. There are no wrong questions.

02
Mention your medications

Supplements interact with many common medications. Your pharmacist can flag anything concerning.

03
Get a personalised answer

What is right for someone you follow online may not be right for you. Your pharmacist knows the difference.
